It all started one fateful day in October 2019. Annie and Billy, two bright eyed technical engineers, were sent by their employer, Epic Systems, on a trip to the University of Texas San Antonio to revolutionize the healthcare industry one medical record software deployment at a time.

Annie distinctly remembers seeing sweet sweet Billy at the Madison airport. He was sitting among a crowd of people at the gate, looking a bit disheveled, and was probably wearing the blue hoodie that has been going strong (or not so strong any more) since middle school, staring blankly into the distance. She remembers thinking he looked like a cute lost puppy... and a little derpy.

The pair jet off to UT San Antonio. There, they meet with their customer counterparts, crushing issue after issue. Annie didn’t think much of the lost puppy again, until the Epic crew was taken out to lunch. Annie sat across from this flouncy-haired boy, who she now learned was called William, and watched him from across the table (in a cool, non creepy way), as he chatted with his colleague. The event was unremarkable, until she saw Billy smile. Ear to ear, crinkles reaching his eyes, exuding warmth like a fresh cup’a tea. She remembers thinking ”oh he’s cute” (momentarily forgetting about the boy she was seeing back in Madison).

They eventually get to talking. Billy mentions he’s headed to Austin for the weekend, and surprisingly enough, so was Annie! When Billy learns that Annie was planning to take the bus, his chivalry kicks in and says nonsense, my brother can drive us.

So there begins Annie and Billy’s first adventure: riding with James down the I-35 through Texas Hill Country.

By this point of the trip, Annie was developing a crush. Billy was kind to his younger brother. He had an easy going nature, good sense of humor, and the cheekbones of a Nordic god. Upon arriving in Austin, the crew took a pit stop at Billy’s parents home to drop off James before Billy shuttled Annie to her Aunt Marilyn and Uncle Chris’ house in North Austin. She met the Caldwell family’s golden retriever Charlie (RIP), a cool guy named John who was about to head out to a live music concert, and a bubbly British lady named Ingrid. She thought “what a lovely family”.

Billy drives Annie to her Aunt and Uncle’s house. Annie tries desperately to entice this mysterious man with her lively conversation, but he was a man of few words. When they get to their destination, Annie, in a moment of uncharacteristic extrovertideness decides to ask Billy if he wanted to come to dinner with her and her Aunt and Uncle.

And here is where you will hear wildly different stories from Annie versus Billy. Their opposing recollection of this moment may be science’s strongest argument for the many worlds interpretation of quantum mechanics. According to Annie, after asking him to dinner, Billy stutters, fumbles, hesitates, hmmphs, and eventually ekes out an uncomfortable “uhhhhhhhh”. And Annie, unable to bear the pain of rejection, rushes out the car and says “oh never mind it’s late you should probably get home, thanks for the ride!”. While in Billy’s alternate reality, Billy confidently responds with an enthusiastic “YES” accompanied with his famous grin, which was unexpectedly met with Annie’s response of “oh never mind it’s late you should probably get home, thanks for the ride!”.

We may never know what actually happened that day in the car.

After their Texas adventure, the two make their way back to Madison. Annie attempts to flirt with Billy in the airport, and even offers him Oreos, as Billy remains oblivious.

Coming home, Annie has a lot to think about. She realizes the guy she was seeing was not the one, given her newfound fascination with Billy, and breaks it off.

After a few weeks of waffling, she eventually gains the confidence to text Billy to ask him to get drinks. And Billy, the self proclaimed “master of timing”, waits a full FOUR HOURS before responding with “I have plans. I’d be up for another time though.”

Thank goodness our heroine had the wherewithal to push past this tepid response, or else we wouldn’t be here seven years later planning this wedding. Eventually the pair identified November 18, 2019 as the date of choice… and the rest is history!

FAQs

What is the dress code for the ceremony?

We truly don’t care what you wear. Annie will be wearing a wedding dress. Billy…who knows.

But if you are craving some guidance, wear clothes that you would wear to a nice dinner. Think casual dresses, jumpsuits, slacks, button downs, and light jackets for the cooler evening weather. Any color is fine. The ceremony will be outdoor on the grass (weather permitting), so we recommend flat shoes.

The average temperature in Madison on September 6 is a high of 77F and a low of 56F (or 25C and 13C for the Brits).

What other clothes should I bring?

Casual, comfortable clothes. A swimsuit if you want to kayak or use the sauna at Seven Acre Dairy. A sun hat if you’re a ginger. Diapers if you're not yet potty trained (or have since lost the ability).

Can I bring a plus-one?

We've kept the guest list small. Your invitation will name everyone we've invited from your party — including any plus-ones and children — and we kindly ask that you join us with just those listed.

Will the ceremony be indoors or outdoors?

The ceremony will be outdoors at Seven Acre Dairy, weather permitting. Dinner will be indoors at the same venue.

Is there parking at the venue?

Yes — parking is available on-site at Seven Acre Dairy.

What's the plan for Saturday?

If you're in town, we'd love to see you! We'll keep you posted on the details as we get closer to the date, but we'll likely spend most of our time hanging out in Paoli. There is kayaking, a sauna, outdoor spaces for relaxing, and art shops. The Taste of Madison will also be going on downtown on the Capitol Square at 2pm, so we might grab a bite to eat there if we're willing to brave the crowds.
